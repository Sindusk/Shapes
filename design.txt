--- Shapes Game ---

Browser-Based Client
- React
- Phaser 3
- Socket.IO

Backend Node.JS Server
- Express
- Socket.IO
- Game Loop (30 ticks/second)
- Prisma
- PostgreSQL

Node, React, Prisma, and PostgreSQL are already installed.
Phaser and Socket are probably not installed.
The game will be hosted on shapes.consistencykings.com.
Users will be shared with other applications on the domain via the PostgreSQL database table "users".

--- General Game Concept ---

A 5x5 grid is presented. Each player has an avatar that takes up one tile.
- Only one player may be on a tile at a time. There is collision, and attempts to move to an occupied tile will be denied.
- Players can move horizontally and vertically. There will not be diagonal movement.
- A boss will be at the top of the screen. It will begin channeling an ability at certain intervals.
- At the start of the channel, tiles will start to glow. At the end of the channel, any player in those tiles will be killed.
- Players have 3 lives. When they run out of lives, they are eliminated.

--- Development Phase 1 ---

Basic Node.JS server implementation where the page loads properly.
Client able to connect and have an emoji or shape appear on the page, and able to move with WASD or arrow keys.

--- Development Phase 2 ---

Add a "boss" mob at the top of the screen. Every few seconds, it will begin a cast bar.
5 random tiles will glow when the cast bar is channeling. If a player ends in one of those tiles, they lose a life.
Display lives for each player on the left side of the screen. Each player starts with 3.

--- Development Phase 3 ---

Increase grid size to 9x9.
Change the highlight of targeted tiles to white instead of red, and give them a soft blinking effect.
Add the concept of matches and rounds to the game. A round is one attack by the boss. A match is as many rounds as it takes until only one player remains.
- If there are no players connected, pause boss attack logic.
- Once one player connects, give a 15 second countdown. Anyone who connects within those 15 seconds will join the current match. Once the countdown completes, begin the rounds.
- Any player who connects while a match is in session will be "benched" until the match concludes. Make sure that they receive a text notification saying so.
- A match concludes when all players are eliminated, including the final one. We'll be making sure matches don't go too long through the next section.
- Timer of the current match should be visible to everyone.
- Eliminations should have timestamp of how long they were alive on the sidebar instead of just "Eliminated"
- Players on the sidebar should be sorted leaderboard-style dynamically, with players with the most lives at the top, and players who are eliminated fastest going to the bottom.
Change default boss timers, and then begin scaling the difficulty. The following formulas should be used for the boss cast time and channel time:
progress = elapsed / maxMatchLength;
castInterval =
    lerp(7.0, 2, progress * progress);
channelTime =
    lerp(5.0, 0.5, progress * progress);
By default, max match length will be 8 minutes.
Additionally, the boss will now attack a random amount of squares on each attack, but it will operate on a bell curve of randomness with the ability to hit any amount of tiles on each roll.
- At the start of the match, the bell curve will have 1/3 of the total tiles be attacked on average.
- By the end of the match, 2/3 of the tiles should be attacked on average.
When the max match length is exceeded, all tiles on the board will be hit with every attack. This will mark "enrage" and forcibly end the match after a few attacks.
